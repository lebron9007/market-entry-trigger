How this works▾

Each indicator below is evaluated independently against a fixed rule. When all five conditions are met simultaneously — a rare event — the historical base rate for positive 12-month forward returns in the S&P 500 is approximately 80–85%, based on retrospective analysis of major historical alignments (e.g. 2008–2009, March 2020).

This dashboard surfaces the framework's current state. It does not make recommendations and is not investment advice.

The five conditions▾

Each indicator's rule and known limitations.

1. VIX > 30

Trigger when CBOE VIX closes above 30.

Limitation: A daily spike can occur for idiosyncratic reasons (single-stock events, earnings season). A sustained reading carries more signal than a one-day move.

2. Fed rate trajectory

Trigger when no future-year FOMC dot-plot median sits above the current target rate.

Limitation: Rate updates 8×/year on FOMC dates; dot plot refreshes only 4×/year (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec). Reflects FOMC participants' expectations, not market pricing.

3. Margin debt decreasing

Trigger when FINRA monthly debit balances are lower than the prior month.

Limitation: Lagging indicator — FINRA publishes ~3 weeks after month-end. Monthly granularity misses intra-month deleveraging.

4. Clear leading sector

Trigger when one sector ETF outperforms SPY by at least 3pp over 3 months and leads the runner-up by 3pp.

Limitation: 3-month window; momentum can reverse quickly. Identifies that capital is flowing somewhere — does not validate the underlying thesis.

5. Leading sector earnings

Trigger when ≥60% of the leading sector's top-10 constituents beat their consensus EPS estimate in the most recent quarter.

Limitation: Quarterly cadence; constituents don't all report in the same quarter. Backward-looking — beating last quarter does not predict the next.
